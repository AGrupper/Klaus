### Critical Review Finding — Resolved

`30.5-REVIEW.md` (2026-07-19T13:13:19Z) found **1 Critical** (CR-01): the D-14 "always-stream
Anthropic" fix (`8be61f3`) replaced `messages.create` with `messages.stream(...)` but only mapped
`anthropic.APIStatusError`/`APIConnectionError` to `LLMError` — a mid-stream `httpx.ReadTimeout`
(the SDK does not wrap transport errors raised while iterating the SSE body) would have bypassed
the entire Sonnet→Gemini→Haiku fallback chain on the highest-traffic path (conversational turns).

**Verified fixed** in commit `688c010` (`core/llm_client.py:280-289`): a new
`except httpx.HTTPError as exc: raise LLMError(...)` clause now catches mid-stream transport
errors, matching the reviewer's prescribed fix exactly. `tests/test_llm_client.py` gained a test
asserting a `httpx.ReadTimeout` raised from `stream.get_final_message()` is mapped to `LLMError`,
plus a companion test confirming a non-httpx callback exception (`TurnCancelled`) still propagates
untouched. Both pass.

### Residual Code-Review Findings (Non-Blocking)

`30.5-REVIEW.md`'s 4 Warnings and 9 Info items remain open (only the 1 Critical, CR-01, was fixed
post-review). None of them fails a documented must-have or ROADMAP success criterion for this
phase; they are flagged here for visibility and future hardening, not as phase-blocking gaps:

- **WR-01** (`core/heartbeat.py:1001-1026`): the daily-spend tripwire send has no quiet-hours gate — per-date suppression makes it deterministically fire around 00:00, inside the 22:00-07:00 quiet window. Explicitly a scoped-out decision recorded in `30.5-05-SUMMARY.md` ("plan's must_haves cover per-date suppression only"), not an oversight — but a real nighttime-push risk.
- **WR-02** (`core/heartbeat.py:807-855`): `check_daily_spend`'s blocking Sonnet compose runs directly on the event loop inside the heartbeat cron — same failure class as the 2026-06-24 weekly-review 500 incident (fixed there via `run_in_executor`), not yet applied here.
- **WR-03** (`core/main.py:630-685`): the 3-tier chain re-attempts a dead primary on every tool-loop iteration rather than sticking to the fallback for the rest of the turn — a slow-mode primary outage can stretch a tool-heavy turn to tens of minutes before `CONNECTIVITY_ERROR_TEXT`.
- **WR-04** (`core/pricing.py:51,64-68`): `_pricing_for` only exact-matches `"claude-sonnet-5"` — a future dated snapshot pin (e.g. `claude-sonnet-5-20260601`) would silently meter $0, defeating both BRAIN-02 and the BRAIN-04 tripwire. Not currently triggered (deploy.yml uses the bare model string), but a latent landmine if the model is ever pinned to a snapshot ID.
- **IN-01 through IN-09**: lower-severity items (tertiary shares primary's API key/failure domain, SELF.md Model Map omits the tertiary tier, `UserProfileStore` negative-cache/shallow-copy edge cases, morning-briefing composes meter as "unknown" purpose, frozen pricing-dict aliasing, failed-tripwire re-billing, D-12 disclosure edge cases, hardcoded cron count). Full detail in `30.5-REVIEW.md`.

Recommend tracking WR-01/WR-02/WR-04 as near-term follow-ups (WR-02 in particular mirrors a
pattern that has already caused a production incident once) — candidates for Phase 35 hardening
or an ad-hoc fix, at Amit's discretion.
